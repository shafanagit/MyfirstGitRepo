# Endemic Animals
![phillippine eagle](https://github.com/shafanagit/MyfirstGitRepo/blob/main/images/phillippine%20eagle.webp?raw=true)

An **endemic animal** is a species that is found **only in a specific geographic area** and nowhere else in the world. Because of this limited range, endemic animals are often especially important for biodiversity—and also more vulnerable to extinction.

### Key points

* **Restricted habitat:** They live in one country, island, or ecosystem.
* **Unique evolution:** They often develop distinct traits due to isolation (linked to Evolution by natural selection).
* **High vulnerability:** Habitat loss, climate change, or invasive species can easily threaten them.

### Examples

* The Sri Lankan leopard — found only in Sri Lanka
* The Galápagos tortoise — native to the Galápagos Islands
* The Koala — endemic to Australia

### Why they matter

Endemic animals help define the identity of a region’s ecosystem. Protecting them also protects many other species and the overall environment.
